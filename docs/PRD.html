1. Executive Summary

TechToons is an interactive, browser-based coloring book that introduces children (ages 4–10) to technology concepts through creative play. Children color tech-themed illustrations — robots, circuit boards, AI brains, rockets, drones, and VR headsets — using intuitive digital tools, building familiarity with STEM imagery while exercising creativity.

Launch Market: Kenya is chosen as the initial market due to its rapidly growing EdTech sector, high mobile internet penetration (~90% via mobile), a young population (over 40% under age 15), and the government's emphasis on digital literacy through the CBC (Competency-Based Curriculum) framework.

Business Goal: Transform the existing prototype into a commercially viable EdTech product targeting Kenyan families and schools, generating recurring revenue through an affordable freemium model priced for the Kenyan market, before expanding across East Africa and beyond.

5. Feature Requirements

5.1 Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–3)

FR-1: User Accounts P0

Parents create accounts; children have sub-profiles. Supports phone number sign-up (common in Kenya) + Google OAuth. Kenya DPA 2019 compliant.

FR-2: Cloud Save P0

Auto-save every 30s + on page leave. Offline support with sync-on-reconnect (critical for intermittent Kenyan internet).

FR-3: Expanded Content Library P0

Grow to 20+ pages across themed collections. Difficulty levels: Easy, Medium, Challenging. 3 free pages + paywall.

FR-4: Freemium Subscription (M-Pesa) P0

Free Tier: 3 pages, basic tools
TechToons Plus: KES 250/mo (~$1.99) — all pages, cloud save, export
TechToons Classroom: KES 500/mo (~$3.99) — class management, 30 students
Payment: M-Pesa via Daraja API (primary) + Stripe for card payments

5.2 Phase 2 — Engagement (Months 4–6)

FR-5: Learning Sidebars P1

3–5 tech facts per page. Read-aloud audio for younger children. "Quiz Me" multiple-choice questions.

FR-6: Achievements P1

Badges, streak tracking, virtual sticker rewards, celebratory animations.

FR-7: Parent Dashboard P1

Session time, pages completed, quiz scores. Weekly SMS/email summary. Screen time limits.

FR-8: Sharing Gallery P2

Moderated community gallery. WhatsApp sharing for family. No PII displayed.

5.3 Phase 3 — Scale (Months 7–12)

FR-9: Android App P1

Android-first (95% of Kenyan mobile). Touch gestures, offline mode, push notifications.

FR-10: Classroom Tools P1

Join codes, page assignments, CBC Digital Literacy strand alignment tags.

FR-11: Printable Worksheets P2

A4 PDF exports with line art and fun facts for device-free sessions.

FR-12: Partner Integrations P2

Embeddable widget for partner sites. API key auth. Revenue share tracking.

Total KES 7.3M (~$56,700)

Pricing insight: KES 250/mo is below a single KFC meal (~KES 600). Global competitors like Toca Boca (KES 650/mo) and ABCmouse (KES 1,700/mo) are unaffordable for most Kenyan families. M-Pesa removes payment friction for 90%+ of adults.
